The Pro-D Jamboree


The Pro-D Jamboree is the only recruiting tournament hosted in British Columbia for teams to showcase their talent. University coaches from all across North America attend the event in hopes of recruiting some of BC's top student-athletes. Pro Caliber Lacrosse aims to provide a high-quality opportunity for all in attendance.


Hosted at one of the most accomodating venues in British Columbia, the Pro-D Jamboree takes advantage of a provincial-wide day off of school to provide players with a unique opportunity to play games and potentially earn scholarships without hindering their academic progress.


Event Format & Features

  • 4 game guaranteed with potential for more via playoffs!
  • Champion crowned per division
  • All-Star Game for the top High School players
  • Games are filmed
  • Travel to Beautiful British Columbia

Dates

October 23rd - 25th, 2020 (Friday, Saturday & Sunday)


Location

TBD


Eligibility

  • High School: 2021 – 2024 Grad Years
  • Youth: 2025 – 2027 Grad Years

Price

High School :: $2,500 per team

Youth Divisions :: $1,750 per team

Free Agent :: $175 per player

**$500 deposit will save your teams' spot – this event sold out last year**


Contact

To Enter a Team or for general inquiries, contact: Sandy Pace - Event Manager sandy@procaliberlacrosse.net

FIELD LACROSSE DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM


The Development Program provides players with a fun, inclusive environment where they can learn lacrosse from young, experienced, world-class coaches who are eager toshare their knowledge with the next generation of athletes. We will be providing two consecutive 8-week, 14-hour programs with no contractual obligation to do both.


Players will see growth in their game by learning a variety of skills ranging from footworkand communication to fundamental stick skills and high-level shooting techniques. Theywill gain a better understanding of lacrosse strategies, including offensive X's & O's and defensive slide packages.


Each player will receive monthly progress reports, healthy meals/snacks/supplements suggestions and a copy of eight dynamic workouts that we will coach them through at the development sessions. We will also host an optional, themed Zoom meeting bi-weekly to build relationships between players and coaches, watch film, do chalk-talk, etc..


The pedigree of our coaching staff is our separating factor. Team Canada, PLL, NLL, MLL, NCAA, our staff has not only done it all, but they continue to do it. With 4-6 coaches present at every session, each of whom brings a unique skillset and flair to coaching, we can provide different perspectives on what it takes to be a successful lacrosse player.

2021-2022 FIELD PROGRAM


The goal of the Travel Program is to provide aspiring student-athletes with opportunities to earn athletic/academic scholarships. Our staff is in constant communication with Universities all across North America who are eager to recruit PCL's athletes.


We will host 4 hours of tryouts to evaluate and select the top team made up of 16 players and 2 goalies.Tryout fee - $25


The team selected will be put to the test preparing for a heavily recruitedBaltimore tournament in an intense eight week, 14-hour program where they will learn how to become a more appealing recruit by preparing for college lacrosse on and off the field.


Each player will receive monthly progress reports, healthy meals/snacks/supplements suggestions and an 8-week workout plan. We will also host an optional, themed Zoom meeting bi-weekly to build relationships between players and coaches, speak with special guests, learn to create highlight tapes, draft letters to college coaches, do chalk-talk, etc..


The boys will also have the option to join in via Zoom to Scholastic Aptitude Test (SAT) preparation classes taught by a high-school teacher who played NCAA soccer and who understands strategies to score more successfully on the test.


Parents will receive a breakdown of what the recruiting process looks like, scholarship expectations, information on the NCAA Clearing House, NCAA recruiting rules and information (DI, DII, DIII), and how to help their sons during what may become a hectic and emotional time. We ask that parents provide us with monthly academic progress reports from their son's councillorso that we can do a better job of helping find Universities that fit their combination of athletic and academicabilities.


The pedigree of our coaching staff is our biggest separating factor. Team Canada, PLL, NLL, MLL, NCAA, our staff has not only done it all, but they continue to do it. With 4-6 coaches present at every session, each having extensive coaching experience in their own rights and who brings a unique skillsetand approach to the game, we can provide different perspectives on what it takes to be a successful lacrosse player.


Since the high-level recruiting events take place during the NLL season, many of our coaches are unable to attend travel events. With this in mind, two "travelling coaches" will work alongside our current pros at every session. The team will travel with those twocoaches as well as two managers who will have their travel expenses covered by program fees.


Our annual "Pro-D Jamboree" is still in question with the status of COVID-19. If we are able to host the event, the team will participatein front of recruiters as a "warm-up" to build in-game chemistry leading up to Baltimore.

2023-2024 FIELD PROGRAM


The goal of this Travel Program is to prepare players for the recruiting process and to provide aspiring student-athletes with opportunities to earn athletic/academic scholarships. Our staff is in constant communication with Universities all across North America who are eager to recruit PCL's athletes.


We will host 4 hours of try-outs to evaluate and select the top team made up of 16 players and 2 goalies. Try-out fee - $25


The team selected will be put to the test in an intense eight week, 14-hour program preparing for one of two heavily recruited tournaments which will be decided upon by the parents, players coaches and program directors. Before a decision is made, the pros and cons of both events will be laid out by the program director, Tyler Pace. Each player will learn how to become a more appealing recruit by preparing for college lacrosse on and off the field.


Every player will receive monthly progress reports, healthy meals/snacks/supplements suggestions and an 8-week workout plan. We will also host an optional, themed Zoom meeting bi-weekly to build relationships between players and coaches, speak with special guests, learn to create highlight tapes, do chalk-talk, etc..


The boys will also have the option to join in via Zoom to Scholastic Aptitude Test (SAT) preparation classes taught by a high-school teacher who played NCAA soccer and who understands strategies to score more successfully on the test.


Parents will receive a breakdown of what the recruiting process looks l ike, scholarship expectations, information on the NCAA Clearing House, and NCAA recruiting rules and information (DI, DII, DIII). We ask that parents provide us with monthly academic progress reports from their son's councillor so that we can do a better job of helping to work with each individual based on their athletic and academic abilities.


The pedigree of our coaching staff is our most significant separating factor. Team Canada, PLL, NLL, MLL, NCAA, our staff has not only done it all, but they continue to do it. With 4-6 coaches present at every session, each having extensive coaching experience in their own rights and who brings a unique skillset and approach to the game, we can provide different perspectives on what it takes to be a successful lacrosse player.


Since the high-level recruiting events take place during the NLL season, many of our coaches are unable to attend travel events. With this in mind, two "travelling coaches" will work alongside our current pros at every session. The team will travel with those twocoaches as well as two managers who will have their travel expenses covered by program fees.


Our annual "Pro-D Jamboree" is still in question with the status of COVID-19. If we are able to host the event, the team will participate in front of recruiters as a "warm-up" to build in-game chemistry leading up to the travel tournament.
